# Volume II: Human Nature

## Purpose

This volume studies the human actor that HODLXXI must actually serve, constrain, and
protect.

A technically correct system that misunderstands human nature will fail.

## Core question

What kind of actor is a human being when promises, memory, shame, loyalty, money,
status, resentment, family, and meaning are involved?

## Scope

This volume studies bounded, emotional, narrative-driven, status-sensitive, tribal,
loyal, resentful, grateful, sacrificial, and deceptive human behavior.

It is not a psychology textbook. It exists to prevent HODLXXI from building mechanisms
that are technically valid but socially naive.

## What this volume claims

- Humans are not only utility maximizers.
- Humans often act through status, shame, loyalty, envy, gratitude, fear, resentment, love, and meaning.
- Commitments are interpreted through story, role, context, and relationship, not only through formal proof.
- Trust systems can become status theater if they reward visible signals without real behavioral constraint.
- Reputation systems can trigger envy, humiliation, cartel formation, or moral capture.
- Forgiveness and repair require human meaning, not only database state.

## What this volume does not claim

- It does not claim humans are irrational noise.
- It does not claim emotions are errors.
- It does not claim incentives are sufficient to explain behavior.
- It does not claim signed commitments are sincere commitments.
- It does not claim public verification prevents manipulation.
- It does not claim shame, honor, or loyalty can be safely reduced to scores.

## Runtime relevance

This volume constrains interpretation of HODLXXI mechanisms such as:

- Human Proof;
- proof of funds;
- public reputation telemetry;
- visible receipts;
- covenant declarations;
- marketplace listings;
- public trust pages;
- future dispute and repair flows.

The runtime can show evidence, but humans may use evidence for performance, signaling,
manipulation, or laundering.

## Failure modes

- Users treat proof of funds as proof of virtue.
- Paid actions become status performance rather than commitment.
- Receipts become symbolic laundering for low-quality behavior.
- Reputation telemetry creates humiliation, cartel behavior, or social caste.
- Forgiveness becomes a ritual reset without behavioral change.
- Public proof increases manipulation rather than trust.

## Falsification focus

This volume fails wherever its model of the human actor causes HODLXXI to reward
symbolic performance instead of durable commitment.

It also fails wherever it treats human meaning as a decorative layer rather than as part
of the system's actual behavior.
