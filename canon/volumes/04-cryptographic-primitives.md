# Volume IV: Cryptographic Primitives

## Purpose

This volume studies cryptographic tools as instruments for memory, accountability, public verification, and durable commitment.

It does not treat trust as authentication.

## Initial themes

- Digital signatures
- Bitcoin
- Lightning
- Covenants
- Hashes
- Proofs
- Attestations
- Receipts
- Public verification
- Timekeeping

## Working premise

Cryptography can make commitments visible and verifiable, but it cannot by itself preserve human meaning.

## Falsification focus

This volume fails wherever cryptographic proof creates ritual without real constraint, identity without continuity, or verification without trustworthiness.
