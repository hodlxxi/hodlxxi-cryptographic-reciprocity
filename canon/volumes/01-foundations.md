# Volume I: Foundations

## Purpose

This volume defines the basic objects of Cryptographic Reciprocity Theory.

It asks what trust, identity, memory, promise, obligation, time, reputation, forgiveness, and legitimacy mean before they become software mechanisms.

## Initial chapters

- Trust
- Identity
- Memory
- Promise
- Obligation
- Time
- Reputation
- Forgiveness
- Legitimacy

## Working premise

Trust is not a static property. It emerges through repeated interaction, remembered behavior, constraints, and costly signals over time.

## Falsification focus

This volume should be considered weak wherever its definitions fail to predict observable behavior in HODLXXI runtime, covenant practice, or long-running human-agent interaction.
