# Volume III: Repeated Games

## Purpose

This volume studies cooperation across repeated interaction.

It asks how reciprocal behavior can emerge, stabilize, degrade, or collapse over time.

## Initial themes

- Iterated strategic interaction
- Reciprocal strategy
- Delayed reciprocity
- Asymmetric reciprocity
- Coalitions
- Trust networks
- Trust collapse
- Strategy evolution

## Working premise

Reciprocity becomes more durable when memory, incentives, identity continuity, and credible constraints make behavior legible across time.

## Falsification focus

This volume fails wherever cryptographically remembered commitments produce brittle escalation, cartel behavior, performative cooperation, or lower long-term fitness than simpler social norms.
