# Proof

## Definition

Proof is evidence that satisfies a defined verification rule.

In CRT, proof is always bounded by the rule it satisfies. A valid proof does not
automatically prove every meaning people may attach to it.

## Operational meaning

A proof must answer:

- What claim is being checked?
- What evidence is being evaluated?
- What rule decides validity?
- Who or what can verify it?
- What remains unproven?

## What proof is not

Proof is not trust.

Proof is not full identity.

Proof is not moral legitimacy.

Proof is not obligation fulfillment unless obligation fulfillment is the explicit claim
being checked.

## Observable behavior

In HODLXXI, proof may appear as:

- signature verification;
- receipt verification;
- requester proof;
- proof of funds;
- event-chain continuity checks;
- covenant script verification.

Each proof must carry its non-claims.

## Failure modes

- The proof is valid but the claim is too narrow for the user's interpretation.
- Users mistake proof of key control for proof of identity.
- Users mistake proof of payment for proof of commitment.
- Users mistake proof of event for proof of obligation fulfillment.
- A proof becomes ritual without behavioral constraint.

## Implications for HODLXXI

Every HODLXXI proof surface should expose what it proves and what it does not prove.

A proof should be treated as a bounded instrument of memory and accountability, not as a
universal trust token.

## Open questions

- Which proof types need user-facing non-claims?
- Should proof claims be machine-readable?
- How should proof scope be displayed to ordinary users?
- When does a proof need external anchoring?
