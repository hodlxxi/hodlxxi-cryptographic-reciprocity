# Identity

## Definition

Identity is continuity of agency across time.

## Why it matters

CRT treats identity as more than login or key possession. Identity matters because
promises, obligations, reputation, and forgiveness require continuity.

## Observation

Identity appears when past actions and future expectations are attributed to the same
actor.

## Counterexamples

A public key is not the whole identity. It can support identity by providing continuity,
but it does not explain motive, role, legitimacy, or responsibility.

A username is not identity if it cannot preserve accountable continuity.

## Historical analogies

Names, seals, signatures, family lineage, legal personhood, merchant houses,
institutional offices, and Bitcoin keys all provide different continuity mechanisms.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should treat public keys as continuity anchors, not as full human identity.
Runtime should distinguish key control, operator continuity, delegated authority, and
social meaning.

## Open questions

When does an agent remain the same agent after model, memory, operator, or policy
changes?

Can identity be delegated without being dissolved?
