# Authority

## Definition

Authority is the recognized capacity to bind, represent, judge, or constrain behavior within a defined scope.

In CRT, authority is not the same as power, capability, signature validity, or possession of a key.

## Operational meaning

Authority becomes operational when the system can answer:

- who grants or recognizes it;
- what scope it covers;
- what evidence supports it;
- what limits constrain it;
- how it can be challenged;
- how it can expire or be revoked.

## What authority is not

Authority is not mere capability.

Authority is not mere fluency.

Authority is not mere control of a public key.

Authority is not reputation alone.

Authority is not legitimacy unless it is recognized within a meaningful context.

## Observable behavior

In HODLXXI, authority may appear through:

- operator identity surfaces;
- agent capabilities;
- signed statements;
- inter-agent messages;
- requester proofs;
- marketplace listings;
- covenant declarations;
- future delegation records.

## Failure modes

- Capability is mistaken for authority.
- A signed message is treated as legitimate representation.
- An agent acts beyond its intended scope.
- Authority is declared but not recognized.
- Authority cannot be revoked.
- Users trust an agent because it is useful rather than because it is bounded.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should avoid granting broad implicit authority to agents.

Authority should be scoped, visible, auditable, and revocable wherever possible.

## Open questions

- Who can grant authority to an agent?
- What is the minimum evidence of authority?
- How should authority expire?
- How should authority be revoked?
- What actions should require explicit renewed authority?
