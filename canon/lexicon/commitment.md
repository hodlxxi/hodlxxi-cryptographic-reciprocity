# Commitment

## Definition

A commitment is a promise made costly to abandon.

## Why it matters

CRT studies whether durable cooperation improves when promises are supported by visible
constraints, capital, memory, and future consequences.

## Observation

Commitment appears when an actor voluntarily limits future options so that others can
rely more safely on expected behavior.

## Counterexamples

A statement without cost is weak commitment.

A lock without human meaning may be empty ritual.

A forced constraint is not the same as a legitimate commitment.

## Historical analogies

Marriage vows, collateral, hostage exchange, guild bonds, escrow, legal surety, Bitcoin
timelocks, and covenant-like scripts all make exit more costly.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should record not only that a commitment exists, but what behavior it is meant
to constrain and what failure would mean.

## Open questions

How costly must a commitment be to become credible?

Can a commitment be too costly, creating coercion rather than trust?
