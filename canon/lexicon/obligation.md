# Obligation

## Definition

An obligation is a remembered claim on future behavior that an actor is expected to
honor.

## Why it matters

CRT treats obligation as the social and temporal weight created when promises, benefits,
debts, roles, or commitments persist across time.

## Observation

Obligation appears when failure to act would be interpreted as betrayal, neglect,
default, dishonor, or illegitimacy.

## Counterexamples

Desire is not obligation.

A transaction can end without continuing obligation.

A rule imposed by force may create compliance without legitimate obligation.

## Historical analogies

Debt, inheritance duty, family care, religious vows, fiduciary roles, military command,
and common-law duties all preserve claims across time.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should distinguish voluntary obligation, delegated obligation, inherited
obligation, and imposed obligation.

## Open questions

Can obligations be inherited by agents?

When should obligation expire?

When does obligation become domination?
