# Promise

## Definition

A promise is a declared future-oriented intention that invites another actor to rely on it.

## Why it matters

CRT treats promises as economically and socially meaningful objects because they shape expectations before fulfillment is complete.

## Observation

A promise appears when one actor makes a future behavior legible enough for another actor to plan around it.

## Counterexamples

A statement of preference is not a promise.

A legal contract may contain promises, but the promise is not identical to the legal form.

A cryptographic signature can record a promise without proving sincere intent.

## Historical analogies

Oaths, vows, merchant letters, marriage promises, public pledges, and signed Bitcoin messages all convert private intention into socially visible expectation.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should distinguish between saying, signing, paying, locking, fulfilling, violating, and repairing a promise.

## Open questions

When does a signed statement become a promise?

Can an AI agent make a promise, or only execute delegated commitments?
