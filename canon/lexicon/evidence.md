# Evidence

## Definition

Evidence is retained information that can support, weaken, or revise an interpretation of past behavior.

In CRT, evidence is not the same as truth. It is material for judgment.

## Operational meaning

Evidence becomes useful when it can be:

- inspected;
- linked to an event or actor;
- compared with other evidence;
- challenged;
- interpreted in context;
- used to update memory, reputation, obligation, or legitimacy.

## What evidence is not

Evidence is not automatically proof.

Evidence is not automatically reputation.

Evidence is not automatically legitimacy.

Evidence is not human meaning by itself.

## Observable behavior

In HODLXXI, evidence may appear as:

- signed receipts;
- attestations;
- event-chain entries;
- verified signatures;
- requester proofs;
- payment records;
- covenant declarations;
- proof-of-funds surfaces;
- runtime audit output.

## Failure modes

- Evidence is overread as final judgment.
- Evidence is collected without context.
- Evidence is selectively displayed.
- Evidence is used for status theater.
- Evidence becomes permanent punishment without repair.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should expose evidence boundaries clearly.

A mechanism should say what it makes visible and what it does not prove.

## Open questions

- Which evidence should be public?
- Which evidence should be private or selectively disclosed?
- What evidence should decay?
- What evidence should be correctable?
- What evidence should be externally anchored?
