# Reciprocity

## Definition

Reciprocity is the structured return of benefit, constraint, recognition, or
responsibility across time.

## Why it matters

CRT studies whether reciprocal commitments can stabilize cooperation without relying on
centralized moral authority.

## Observation

Reciprocity appears when an actor's present behavior is shaped by remembered past
treatment and expected future interaction.

## Counterexamples

Immediate exchange is not the full meaning of reciprocity. Delayed, asymmetric, and
indirect return may still be reciprocal.

Revenge can imitate reciprocity while destroying future cooperation.

## Historical analogies

Gift economies, family obligation, rotating credit, merchant networks, and repeated
games all preserve forms of return across time.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should make reciprocal commitments visible without forcing every relationship
into immediate settlement.

## Open questions

When does reciprocity become coercive dependency?

Can asymmetric reciprocity remain stable without becoming exploitation?
