# Consent

## Definition

Consent is a voluntary and meaningful acceptance of a proposed action, relationship,
constraint, or transfer of authority.

In CRT, consent is not the same as silence, technical access, payment, or possession of
a key.

## Operational meaning

Consent becomes operational when the system can represent:

- who consented;
- what they consented to;
- when consent was given;
- what information they had;
- what scope and duration apply;
- whether consent can be withdrawn;
- what evidence supports the consent claim.

## What consent is not

Consent is not mere interface completion.

Consent is not mere payment.

Consent is not mere signature validity.

Consent is not coercion, confusion, or hidden dependency.

Consent is not permanent unless permanence is explicit and legitimate.

## Observable behavior

In HODLXXI, consent may appear through:

- signed requester statements;
- Human Proof paths;
- paid job requests;
- marketplace offers;
- covenant participation;
- future delegation records;
- future acceptance criteria.

## Failure modes

- A user signs without understanding scope.
- Payment is treated as consent to broader terms.
- A key signs but the human context is unclear.
- Consent is captured once and treated as permanent.
- Interface design hides important constraints.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should avoid treating all signed or paid actions as full consent.

Consent should be scoped, legible, and tied to the claim being made.

## Open questions

- What consent needs to be explicit?
- How should consent be revoked?
- How should consent be proven without overexposing private information?
- When is a signature enough?
- When does social context make technical consent inadequate?
