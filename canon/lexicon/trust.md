# Trust

## Definition

Trust is a prediction about future behavior that survives repeated interaction under
uncertainty.

## Why it matters

CRT treats trust as something that emerges over time through memory, incentives,
constraints, and observable conduct.

## Observation

Trust appears when one actor accepts exposure to another actor's future behavior without
full control over the outcome.

## Counterexamples

Authentication is not trust. A valid signature can prove key control without proving
future reliability.

Familiarity is not trust. Repeated contact can coexist with betrayal, dependency, or
manipulation.

## Historical analogies

Merchant credit, kinship obligations, guild membership, common-law reputation, and
Bitcoin confirmations all show different ways of reducing uncertainty over time.

## Implications for HODLXXI

HODLXXI should not reduce trust to login, signature, or payment. Runtime surfaces should
show continuity, constraint, receipts, behavior, and failure history.

## Open questions

How much remembered behavior is enough to support trust?

Which failures should reduce trust permanently, and which should allow recovery?
